🦉 About Conjuga

Conjuga is a free, no-login skills builder for K-5 Spanish Dual Language Immersion (DLI) learners. It targets one high-leverage skill: conjugating the 100 most common Spanish verbs in the present, preterite, and imperfect tenses — practiced five verbs at a time through recognition, production, and matching activities.

Who it's for

  • Novice learners (NCSSFL-ACTFL Novice Low → Novice High), the typical K-5 DLI range.
  • Teachers who want a standards-aligned, zero-setup practice station.
  • Families supporting immersion homework — no account, no personal data collected.

Standards grounding

NBPTS World Languages Standards (ECYA-WL)

The National Board's World Languages standards — the national-level description of accomplished world-language teaching — shape the pedagogy:

  • Std IV (Knowledge of Language Acquisition): recognition before production (Elige → Escribe), high-repetition exposure, and treating errors as part of learning — feedback is corrective, never punitive.
  • Std VI (Designing Curriculum): sequential, spiraling design — 20 frequency-ordered groups of 5 verbs, present before past tenses, "from simple to sophisticated."
  • Std VII (Assessment): clear, timely feedback; star-based mastery tracking that helps students reflect on their own progress.
  • Std V (Fair and Equitable Learning Environment): free, no-login, works on any device, bilingual instructions, accent-key support, reduced-motion and screen-reader support.

NCSSFL-ACTFL Can-Do Statements (proficiency levels)

Proficiency levels come from the NCSSFL-ACTFL Can-Do Statements — a joint framework of the National Council of State Supervisors for Languages (NCSSFL) and the American Council on the Teaching of Foreign Languages (ACTFL). Conjuga targets the Novice band:

  • Novice Low — communicates with memorized words and phrases about very familiar topics.
  • Novice Mid — communicates with memorized words and some phrases about familiar topics.
  • Novice High — communicates with words, lists, and simple sentences; asks and answers questions (DLI learners often reach this early).

🪺 El Nido de Lola (the nest)An unscored celebration layer: every earned star adds to Lola's nest — a first star brings a straw wisp, finishing every activity in a group brings a twig, and a perfect group adds a flower. Nothing is ever lost, nothing is random, and there is no counter of what's missing: the nest is a mirror of growth, never a checklist (NBPTS Std V; self-determination research on competence displays).
🌤️ El Vuelo de Lola (the flight)An optional 60-90 second celebration after any finished round: Lola visibly flies home across the sky, one cloud closer to her nest with every correct answer. With audio it plays by ear — the child taps the cloud carrying the form they hear (interpretive listening, with an ABC button that shows the text for anyone who prefers to read it); without audio it plays as text. The reward itself is more Spanish, never a detour from it: no timer, no failure state, big fixed tap targets, fully playable with reduced motion, and a great score adds sparkle without ever being the price of admission.

Note: full accuracy across time frames is formally an Intermediate-and-above expectation; here the past tenses — and the two unscored "Tiempos nuevos" constructions — are deliberate stretch scaffolding for DLI learners, whose contact hours far exceed typical world-language programs.

Linguistic accuracy: every conjugated form in the app is checked against the Real Academia Española (RAE) conjugation tables through a hand-verified, automated test suite that must pass before any change goes live — including every irregular verb, spelling change, and the new stretch constructions.

Game design, grounded in research

Jugamos para aprender — nunca al revés.

Conjuga's playful touches follow the learning-science evidence for young learners, and we hold every game mechanic to the same rules:

  • Rewards are earned, never random. Nothing is bought, nothing is chance-based — randomized-reward mechanics normalize gambling-style loops in children (Zendle et al.).
  • Celebrations are surprises, not contracts. Expected, performance-contingent prizes are the kind shown to undermine children's intrinsic motivation, while unexpected celebrations of competence do not (Deci, Koestner & Ryan).
  • Nothing decays, resets, or is taken away. No streaks to lose, no leagues, no countdown clocks — loss-framed mechanics are linked to anxiety and performative play in kids (Duolingo gamification study). In the matching game, counts only ever go up: winning runs are celebrated when they happen, and a miss simply says "inténtalo otra vez."
  • More fun always means more Spanish. We design against the documented failure mode where a game wrapper outcompetes the learning (Fairplay's Prodigy complaint).
  • Built for small hands. Big fixed tap targets, no precision dragging, no long-presses, no time pressure — matching what motor-skills research shows young children can actually do on touchscreens (Nielsen Norman Group).
  • Every child's effort counts. The nest grows from the very first star — a reward that only perfect scores could reach would celebrate the students who least need it and exclude the ones trying hardest. Perfection upgrades a celebration; it never gates one (world-building reward loops modeled on Teach Your Monster to Read).

Para maestros / For teachers

Cada grupo tiene un 🍎 Pack de clase — hojas de práctica en blanco para los cinco tiempos (los tres centrales y los dos de exploración) más sus claves de respuesta, en una sola impresión — y una tarjeta de estación con código QR que abre ese grupo directamente en una tableta. Open any group and tap "🍎 Para maestros: Pack de clase": one print job yields blank practice sheets for all five tenses with answer keys at the back, plus a QR station card that opens that group directly on a tablet — ideal for classroom stations. No accounts needed on any device; each device keeps its own progress.

Sin conexión / Offline

Conjuga se puede instalar como una app — “Añadir a pantalla de inicio” desde el menú ☰ (📲 Instalar la app) — y funciona sin internet: la aplicación se guarda en el aparato y, con la pantalla ⬇️ Descargas, también el audio de cada grupo (unos 2 MB por grupo). Conjuga installs as an app and works offline — the app itself is stored on the device, and the ⬇️ Descargas screen saves each group's audio locally (about 2 MB per group), including the 🎧 listening activity. Everything stays on the device.

Una nota sobre el progreso: en iPhone y iPad, la app instalada guarda su propio progreso, separado del navegador Safari — así funciona el sistema, no es un error. On iPhone/iPad the installed app keeps its own stars and badges, separate from Safari's — that's how iOS isolates installed web apps, by design. On Android and desktop the browser and the installed app share the same progress. Pick one home for practicing (the installed app is best) and stick with it.

Privacy

No accounts, no login, no third-party trackers, no advertising, no personal data collection. Progress is stored only in this browser's local storage, can be erased with one click, and never leaves the device.

The only information the site ever sends anywhere is an anonymous, aggregate visit count to our own server — a counter that records that a page was viewed, never who viewed it. No IP addresses, no cookies, no identifiers of any kind are stored, and the server's source code is public in our repository. To count how many different devices visit, the server also keeps one-way codes made with secret values that are discarded daily and monthly — codes that cannot be turned back into an address, linked across time, or tied to a person. Counting of this kind is disclosed here in line with the "support for internal operations" allowance of the U.S. children's-privacy rule (COPPA).
