Cómo llegamos aquí · Process

Three parallel research passes fed the ideation: (1) the learning-science evidence on gamification for ages 5–11 (what motivates, what backfires); (2) a codebase & brand analysis (Lola's spec, Prado tokens, scoring tracks, storage schema, payload headroom); (3) browser-game engineering for touch-first vanilla JS. Eight candidate concepts were then attacked by three independent critics — pedagogy, engineering, and K-5 UX/accessibility — and only ideas surviving all three lenses were refined into the finalists below. Notably, all three critics independently converged on the same hybrid.

Las reglas del juego · Evidence-based guardrails

Every finalist obeys these, derived from the research pass (citations at the end):

Deterministic & effort-earned. No randomness, rarity, or chance — mystery boxes are gambling-adjacent; deterministic mystery reveals are the top-ranked engagement element.
Informational, never contractual. Rewards arrive as surprises celebrating what just happened. The UI never says "earn 3 stars to unlock X" — announced performance-contingent prizes crowd out intrinsic motivation most in young children (Deci).
Nothing decays, nothing is lost. No wilting, no hunger, no streak resets, no visible counter a mistake can destroy. Additive worlds only.
The Prodigy test. The fastest route to more fun must be more Spanish. Any toy that outcompetes the learning is cut.
Equity of delight. Rewards reach the child at 1 star, not just the 100% kid. Perfection upgrades celebrations; it never gates access.
K-5 motor & attention reality. Tap-tap on ≥56 px anchored targets (never moving-target taps, no drag precision, no long-press); 60–90 s reward moments inside 3–5 min bursts.
Reduced-motion parity. The game must be the same game with motion replaced by state swaps — never a lesser one.
No economy. No currencies, shops, loot, leaderboards, or streak guilt — the existing non-goal stands; celebration layers derive from stars and are never scored.

Idea 1 · recomendada · la favorita de los tres críticos

El Vuelo al Nido — the flight home & the living nest

Cada ronda termina con un vuelo. Cada estrella construye el nido.

The pitch. Conjuga's one load-bearing metaphor — ayuda a Lola a llegar a su nido — becomes the whole reward system. When a child finishes any scored round, Lola takes a short celebratory flight (60–75 s, always skippable): four or five big clouds hold conjugated forms from the round just played; a prompt speaks a form (audio when available, text pill always) and the child taps its cloud; Lola swoops to the tapped cloud. Wrong clouds simply drift on — no failure state. The flight ends with a landing at the nest, a persistent scene (reached from home) that grows from the child's real mastery data: a straw wisp when a group earns its first star, a twig when every activity in a group has at least one star, a flower on the twig at a perfect 30/30 — that last one delivered as the twig-carried-home cutscene with Lola's signature is-spin. Every nest item is tappable to hear its Spanish name.

ShapeReward mini-game + meta-progression, one metaphor
Scoring trackUnscored celebration layer — zero recordResult
Storage100% derived from existing best — no schema change
PayloadNest ~4–6 KB gz (module) · flight 8–12 KB gz lazy, prefetched
Size4 loop iterations (L)
AudioCloud prompts = existing conjugation clips ✓ · nest nouns need one owner clip run

Why the critics converged on it

  • It passes the Prodigy test outright — the fun toy is recognition-mode Spanish (an Elige/Escucha hybrid wearing a cape), sampling the round's own verbs as covert spaced micro-review.
  • Equity by design. Every finished round flies (celebration scales with stars — more clouds, the loop-de-loop — but access never gates on perfection); every earned star visibly builds the nest. The 28/30 child sees a full, growing nest, not an empty trophy case.
  • Pre-sanctioned. docs/MASCOT.md already names "the nest gaining a twig per fully-starred group" as the future direction; this proposal softens its gate and fills the app's missing set-completion celebration moment.
  • Motor-skill honest. Clouds are anchored ≥64 px buttons that bob in place a few pixels; motion lives in Lola's feedback swoop, not in the targets. Under prefers-reduced-motion only the garnish disappears — the game is pixel-identical.
  • Screen-reader real. The nest is a DOM list of focusable items with a summary sentence ("El nido tiene 7 ramitas"); clouds are real buttons whose accessible names are the forms; reveals narrate via the existing live region.

Mechanics detail

The nest renders only what exists — never empty slots, never an "X/20" counter. It is a mirror of growth, not a checklist (the pedagogy critic's line: safe as a mirror, dangerous as a checklist).

Engineering plan

  • New modules, not mascot growth: js/nido.js (scene + derivation from best) and lazy js/vuelo.js (flight), keeping js/mascot.js under its 8 KB cap — the twig is an SVG overlay near Lola, never a new body pose.
  • Reduced-motion-first build order: the static cloud grid is built and e2e-tested first; drift/swoop garnish layers on top. e2e runs under reducedMotion: 'reduce' and seeds states (29/30 → one perfect round) via addInitScript to assert celebrations deterministically.
  • Lazy + resilient: flight module prefetched post-boot on idle; if the import fails offline, results screen renders as today — the surprise degrades silently, never breaks.
  • Audio honesty: cloud prompts reuse the existing 3,353-clip manifest (they are conjugated forms — the only game concept whose audio already works). Nest nouns (la ramita, la flor…) need a ~15-clip owner ElevenLabs run, or ship TTS-only gated on audioAvailable().
  • Routes: #/nido (a real screen with crumbs/☰/footer per standing rule 5); flight is an overlay state on results, not a route.

Idea 2 · la victoria rápida · ship-first

Empareja con Chispa — make the matching game feel like a game

Cero pantallas nuevas. Cero datos nuevos. Pura chispa.

The pitch. Empareja already has game bones; it's missing juice. Add a satisfying card-flip on selection (decorative CSS only — state classes stay synchronous so existing e2e timing contracts hold), a soft WebAudio chirp on match (two rising sine notes, synthesized — no files, created lazily on first tap for iOS, gated on the sound setting, never the only success signal), Lola's existing is-turn when the child hits a run, and a warm end-of-round moment within the existing ≤900 ms celebration spec. The counter counts up only ("5 parejas"); runs are celebrated when they happen ("¡3 seguidas!") and the UI simply says nothing when they don't — no meter ever visibly resets. The critics' one hard finding, the combo-reset (the app's first would-be punishment mechanic), is deleted.

ShapeGamified existing exercise (juice pass)
Scoring trackUnchanged — ⭐ stars as today
StorageNone
Payload~1–2 KB gz across app.js/css
Size1 loop iteration (S)
AudioWebAudio oscillators — no clips needed

Guardrail notes

  • Mismatch feedback stays deliberately quieter and slower than match feedback — the correction must win attention (MASCOT.md rule), so the sparkle never outshouts the fix.
  • Reduced motion: flips become crossfades; the chirp still plays (audio isn't motion); the visual match state alone must carry meaning for muted/deaf contexts.
  • Live region announces matches, never meter ticks — no aria-live spam.

Idea 3 · la expansión cultural · SME-gated

Las Postales de Lola — a mystery postcard album from the Spanish-speaking world

Cada estrella nueva revela un pedacito del mundo.

The pitch. Lola travels. Postcards from real places — un mercado en Oaxaca, el Viejo San Juan, la Boca en Buenos Aires — arrive as mail at her nest (one world, no new home tile). Each postcard is hidden behind 12 tiles, and a tile is revealed for every new star earned (best-star increases only — re-grinding a mastered set reveals nothing, which defuses the stars-as-currency risk the pedagogy critic flagged, while the UX critic's equity requirement holds: a struggling child's single hard-won star still visibly moves the picture). A finished postcard joins the album with a one-line bilingual caption — a genuine interpretive-culture micro-lesson, tappable to hear — opening Conjuga's first honest on-ramp to the NCSSFL-ACTFL Cultures Can-Dos.

ShapeMeta-reward collection (deterministic mystery reveal)
Scoring trackUnscored — derived from total best-stars
StorageNone — tiles = f(total stars); album = f(threshold crossings)
Payload6 postcards, flat token-colored SVG, 2–4 KB gz each, lazy per-card
Size3 loop iterations (M–L) + SME review gate
AudioCaption clips need an owner ElevenLabs run

Hard conditions (from the critique)

  • SME cultural review before ship — same pattern as the M2/M5 holds. Drive-by culture is worse than none; every scene and caption needs a bilingual educator's eyes. This is why Idea 3 is a later phase, not a first build.
  • Six postcards, not twenty. Hand-drawn SVG scenes are the hidden payload bomb (20 scenes ≈ 60–160 KB). Six flat scenes in Prado tokens (dark mode automatic), lazy-loaded from the album route, stay inside a 15–25 KB total envelope.
  • Non-visual parity: every reveal narrates via the live region ("Postal de Oaxaca: 7 de 12 piezas"); finished postcards carry real alt text and their caption as DOM text.
  • Budget honesty: art must live where the payload test can see it, or the test's scope gets an explicit owner-approved amendment — the loop must never park art in an untested directory to dodge the budget.
  • STANDARDS.md stays honest: the claim added is modest — "exposure to products and practices," never "develops cultural competence."

Cambios que requieren tu firma · Owner sign-offs needed

  • GOAL.md non-goals amendment. The current line excludes "economy-style gamification" and says "stars/streaks remain the only scorekeeping." The finalists respect the spirit (no currencies, shops, loot, leaderboards, or streak mechanics — still banned), but the letter needs one clarifying sentence. Proposed replacement:
    Accounts, servers, dashboards, analytics, other languages (until M5 is done and a human re-scopes), external services, and economy-style gamification (currencies, shops, random loot, competitive leaderboards, streak-guilt mechanics). ⭐ stars and 🎧 badges remain the only scorekeeping; celebration layers (nest, flight, album) must be fully derived from them — deterministic, unscored, and never a new currency.
  • Scoring-track declaration: all three finalists are unscored celebration layers — zero recordResult, zero denominator changes. No new track is created (the "third track allowed" default went unused — derivation beat currency).
  • One ElevenLabs clip run (owner key): ~15 nest nouns for Idea 1; caption lines for Idea 3 when it ships.
  • SME cultural review for Idea 3's scenes and captions (M2/M5 pattern).

Fuentes · Key research

  • Sailer & Homner (2019), The Gamification of Learning meta-analysis — gamification works best via goals + challenge + curiosity, not points/leaderboards; larger effects for elementary learners. springer.com
  • Deci, Koestner & Ryan (1999/2001) — expected tangible performance-contingent rewards undermine intrinsic motivation most in children; unexpected/informational rewards do not. selfdeterminationtheory.org
  • Fairplay FTC complaint re: Prodigy Math — the cautionary tale of the game wrapper outcompeting the learning. fairplayforkids.org
  • Vatavu et al. (2015), touch interaction in young children — taps on big targets and swipes easy; precise drag unreliable; no long-press/multi-touch. sciencedirect.com · NN/g
  • Zendle et al. — randomized reward (loot-box) mechanics normalize gambling patterns in minors; deterministic earned reveals avoid this. pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov
  • Duolingo streak/league research — engagement mechanics vs. learning; streak anxiety in kids. arxiv.org
  • Teach Your Monster to Read — the gold-standard K-5 reward loop (earned customization feeding a world the child owns). Common Sense
  • Schroeder et al., pedagogical-agent meta-analysis — expressive cartoon mascots yield small positive learning/motivation effects. frontiersin.org
  • js13kGames — complete games in 13 KB zipped; calibrates the 15–25 KB module budget as generous. wikipedia.org
